## 2-Minute Pitch (Deep Dive)

"Let me walk you through JustMoney, a product strategy project I built from scratch.

**The Problem**: India has 650 million smartphone users. UPI has gone everywhere — even to Varanasi's sabzi mandis. But financial management? Still analog. I wanted to understand why.

**The Research**: I ran 100+ user interviews and surveys across 4 Tier-2 cities. I recruited working professionals aged 22–45 — government employees, shopkeepers, first-time earners. 78% said apps were in English they didn't understand. 65% said they didn't trust apps with bank data. 71% had no idea where their money went each month.

**The Insight**: These users don't need investment advice. They need a financial mirror. Simple, honest, in their language.

**The Solution**: JustMoney. Three core pillars:
1. Vernacular-first — full Hindi UI, financial terms explained simply
2. Trust-first — 'try without bank linking' option, plain-language privacy explainer
3. Goal-based savings — visual 'buckets' for things that matter (school fees, Diwali shopping, bike)

**The Validation**: I built a Figma prototype and tested it with 20 users. We got a 4.5 out of 5 CSAT. Task completion rate was 85%. The stickiest insight: users spent the most time on the goal buckets and said it was the first time they'd felt 'in control' of their finances.

**The Opportunity**: TAM of ₹28,000 Cr in Indian personal finance apps. No player currently owns the vernacular-first, trust-first Tier-2 segment.

**What I'd do next**: Launch with a B2B2C GTM — employer wellness programs as the trust distribution channel — and track WAU as our North Star metric."

## STAR Story — Research & Insight Discovery

**Situation**: As part of an MBA product strategy course, I chose to explore the personal finance app market for Tier-2 Indian cities — a market I believed was underserved but hadn't been researched deeply.

**Task**: Design a research plan, conduct primary research, synthesize insights, and develop a product strategy with enough depth to present to product and business leaders.

**Action**:
- Designed a 2-part research plan: 60 qualitative interviews + 45 structured surveys
- Recruited participants across Varanasi, Lucknow, Kanpur, Allahabad — targeting 22–45 year olds using UPI but not finance apps
- Conducted interviews exploring financial behaviors, pain points, and app perceptions
- Synthesized findings into 7 validated pain points and 4 user personas
- Applied RICE framework to prioritize features
- Built a Figma prototype and ran usability testing with 20 participants

**Result**: Prototype achieved 4.5/5 CSAT and 85% task completion. Key insight — "trust before bank-linking" — became a core product feature (explore mode). Project received strong peer and faculty recognition as a deeply researched, structured product case study.

---

## STAR Story — Insight to Decision

**Situation**: During prototype testing, 12 out of 20 users hesitated at the bank-linking screen, even after the privacy explainer was shown. This was a potential product-breaker — the dashboard requires bank data to work.

**Task**: Decide whether to (a) improve the privacy messaging, (b) make bank-linking optional, or (c) restructure onboarding entirely.

**Action**:
- Ran 5 quick follow-up interviews with hesitant users
- Root cause: not messaging — it was trust built over time, not in one screen
- Decision: introduce "Explore Mode" — show a demo dashboard with sample data before asking for bank link
- Updated prototype to add Explore Mode → re-tested with 8 users

**Result**: All 8 re-test users said they'd be more comfortable linking their bank after experiencing the demo. Bank-link intent rate jumped from 55% to 82% in re-test cohort.

## PM Interview Q&A

**Q: Walk me through how you'd prioritize features for JustMoney.**

"I used RICE scoring across 6 candidate features. The Trust Explainer scored highest (189) because it has broad reach, high impact on our critical conversion metric (bank-link rate), high confidence from user research, and minimal engineering effort. Monthly Nudge ranked second (149) for similar reasons — high value, low cost. I deprioritized credit scoring (not on the RICE list) entirely for v1 because it triggers regulatory complexity and user anxiety — two things that fight against our core 'trust-first' positioning."

**Q: What's your North Star metric for JustMoney?**

"Weekly Engaged Users — users who complete at least one financial action per week. Not MAU, because in personal finance, monthly active isn't good enough. Not DAU, because daily finance check-ins are unrealistic for our user profile. WAU captures the 'weekly money moment' habit we're trying to build — whether that's checking the dashboard, updating a goal, or acting on a spending nudge."

**Q: How would you measure the success of the Hindi language feature?**

"I'd run an A/B test — English UI for control group, Hindi UI for treatment. Primary metrics: Onboarding completion rate, Day-7 retention, and Bank-link rate. Secondary: Session duration and nudge CTR. Based on our research, I'd expect 20–30% improvement in onboarding completion with Hindi UI, since 78% of users cited English as a barrier."

## Consulting Interview Version

**Frame**: "I analyzed the personal finance app market for Tier-2 India using a structured primary research approach."

**Issue Tree**:
```
Why aren't Tier-2 users adopting personal finance apps?
├── Supply side: Apps not designed for them
│   ├── Language barrier (English-only)
│   ├── UI complexity (too many features)
│   └── Pricing mismatch (premium features, Tier-2 income)
└── Demand side: User barriers
    ├── Trust deficit (bank data safety)
    ├── Awareness gap (never heard of alternatives)
    └── Habit gap (no existing digital finance behavior)
```

**Recommendation**: A vernacular-first, trust-first app addressing supply-side barriers first (language, simplicity) to unlock latent demand.
