# Interview Question Bank — JustMoney
*Aditi Mishra | PM + Consulting + MBA Interview Prep*

---

## PM Interview Questions & Strong Answers

**Q1: How did you decide what features to build for JustMoney?**

Strong Answer: "I used a RICE prioritization framework after completing user research. The key insight was that traditional feature thinking — build what users ask for — would lead to a bloated app. Instead, I focused on the highest-leverage intervention: trust and language. The Trust Explainer scored 189 on RICE — highest of all candidates — because it addressed the #1 barrier (65% of users cited privacy distrust) with minimal engineering effort. That logic drove our v1 scope."

---

**Q2: What's your North Star metric for JustMoney, and why?**

Strong Answer: "Weekly Engaged Users who complete at least one financial action. I rejected MAU because monthly active doesn't capture habit formation — someone who opens the app once per month hasn't changed their financial behavior. I rejected DAU because daily finance check-ins are unrealistic for Tier-2 users. WAU captures the 'weekly money moment' — the kind of recurring engagement that actually changes financial outcomes and drives long-term retention."

---

**Q3: You said CSAT was 4.5/5. What would you do to get it to 5/5?**

Strong Answer: "The 0.5 gap came primarily from the bank-linking friction in testing — 12/20 users hesitated. To close it: First, I'd ship 'Explore Mode' (demo dashboard without bank link) which our re-tests showed dropped hesitancy from 45% to 18%. Second, I'd add progressive trust signals — showing 'Bank-grade encryption', RBI partner badge, and user testimonials specifically at the link step. Third, I'd A/B test the bank-link timing — move it to Day 3 rather than onboarding Day 1, after users have experienced the app's value first."

---

**Q4: How would you handle a situation where your research contradicts what stakeholders want to build?**

Strong Answer: "In the JustMoney project, my initial hypothesis was that users wanted investment features — like SIPs and FDs. When research contradicted this (only 18% mentioned investment as a priority; 78% wanted basic spending visibility), I had to reframe the problem. I'd present the research data directly — the 100+ interviews, the verbatim quotes, the persona pain points. Data is a stakeholder's friend. I'd also propose a phased approach: nail the core use case in v1, then layer investments in v2 when users trust the platform. That's harder to argue against."

---

**Q5: JustMoney has a trust problem. Walk me through how you'd measure trust.**

Strong Answer: "Trust is abstract, so I'd measure it through behavioral proxies:
1. Bank-link adoption rate — the ultimate trust signal (did they give us their bank data?)
2. Permission grant rate — did they allow SMS/notification access?
3. Organic referral rate — do they trust us enough to recommend to family?
4. Support ticket rate — low trust = high complaint rate
5. Qualitative NPS comments — surface the 'trust language' in open-ended responses
And I'd run a trust survey monthly: 'How safe do you feel sharing your financial data with JustMoney?' on a 1–5 scale, tracking trends."

## Product Strategy Questions

**Q6: A competitor launches a Hindi personal finance app tomorrow. What do you do?**

"First, don't panic — language alone is table stakes. Our moat is trust + community. Actions: (1) Accelerate B2B2C employer partnerships — distribution through trusted employers is hard to replicate fast; (2) Deepen the vernacular advantage — go from Hindi to 5 regional languages; (3) Double down on goal-based savings UX — our prototype testing showed this was the most emotionally resonant feature; (4) Analyze competitor's reviews in 90 days to find their failure points and our opportunities."

---

**Q7: Swiggy wants to add a personal finance feature inside their app. Threat or opportunity?**

"More opportunity than threat for a standalone app like JustMoney. Swiggy's in-app finance would likely be superficial — spend analytics tied to food only. Our advantage: complete financial picture, goal-based savings, vernacular-first. But I'd monitor it. If Swiggy's user base overlaps (Tier-2 food-ordering users), they could poach our top-of-funnel. Mitigation: deepen the 'whole financial life' features they can't replicate, and accelerate the employer channel which Swiggy won't touch."

## Metrics Questions

**Q8: Your D30 retention drops from 40% to 25% in Month 3. Walk me through diagnosis.**

"I'd build a retention diagnostic:
1. **Cohort analysis** — is it all cohorts or one specific week's cohort? (product bug vs. structural issue)
2. **Funnel breakdown** — where in the funnel are users dropping? (onboarding? post-goal completion?)
3. **Feature usage correlation** — do low-retention users have common behavior? (e.g., never created a goal)
4. **User interviews** — survey 20 churned users; most valuable input
5. **External factors** — did a competitor launch? Did we push a bad update?
Most likely hypothesis: users who didn't create a goal in Day 1 never formed a habit. Recommendation: implement a Day-3 goal-creation nudge for users who skipped it during onboarding."

## Consulting Questions

**Q9: Estimate the market size for personal finance apps in Tier-2 India.**

"I'd use a bottom-up approach. India has 1.4B people; roughly 600M live in Tier-2/3 cities. Of those, ~50% (300M) are working-age adults. Smartphone penetration in Tier-2 is ~55%, so ~165M smartphone users. Of those, realistic addressable market for a finance app (regular income, basic digital literacy): ~30%, i.e., ~50M users. Willingness to pay ₹99/month: ~15% = 7.5M paying users. At ₹99/month = ₹8,900 Cr ARR potential. That's the premium segment; add a freemium base of 3x = ~22M freemium users on ad/data monetization."

## Behavioral / Leadership Questions

**Q10: Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.**

"During the JustMoney prototype testing, I identified that 12 out of 20 users were hesitating at bank-linking. I had two options: re-run the entire research cycle to get more data, or make a design call based on what I had. With a project deadline, I chose to act. I ran 5 quick follow-up probes (30 minutes each), identified that the issue was trust timing, not messaging, and added the Explore Mode to the prototype. Re-testing showed 18% hesitancy vs. 45% before. Decision made with 70% confidence, validated quickly."
