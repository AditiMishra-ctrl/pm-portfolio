# Executive Summary — JustMoney: Personal Finance App for Tier-2 Cities
*Aditi Mishra | MBA Candidate, IIIT Lucknow | Product Strategy Project*

---

## The Problem

Tier-2 and Tier-3 Indian cities are home to 600M+ people. Despite the UPI revolution, **financial literacy and structured personal finance management remain deeply underserved** in these markets. Users in cities like Varanasi, Kanpur, Agra, and Jhansi face:

- Fragmented financial lives across 3–4 apps (bank app, UPI, EMI tracker, RD tracker)
- Reliance on relatives or bank branch staff for financial decisions
- Low trust in automated advisory tools
- Language and UI complexity that creates adoption friction

## What I Built (Research + Strategy Project)

JustMoney is a **consumer product strategy** for a vernacular-first personal finance app tailored for Tier-2 Indian users. This was a research-driven, ground-up project involving primary user research with 100+ participants.

## Market Opportunity *(Assumption — labeled)*

> **[ASSUMPTION]** Market sizing based on public DPIIT and RBI data.

- **TAM**: ₹28,000 Cr — total addressable personal finance app market in India
- **SAM**: ₹8,400 Cr — Tier-2/3 cities with smartphone penetration >40%
- **SOM**: ₹840 Cr — achievable 10% SAM capture in 5 years for a well-positioned app

## Strategic Recommendation

A **vernacular-first, trust-driven** personal finance app targeting 25–45 year old working professionals in Tier-2 cities — offering:
1. **Unified financial dashboard** (UPI + bank + investments + EMIs)
2. **Goal-based savings buckets** in Hindi + regional languages
3. **Nudge-based financial coaching** (WhatsApp-style, not formal advisory)

## Business Impact

- Reduces financial stress for 600M+ underserved Indians
- Creates a high-retention, high-LTV user base for financial product cross-selling
- Revenue model: freemium + B2B2C (employer financial wellness programs)
